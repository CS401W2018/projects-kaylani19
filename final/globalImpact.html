Samoan Public Figure Impact

In today's generation, the Pacific Islands have increasingly improved their presence both in and outside of the spotlight. According to Samoa Observer, the country's newspaper establishment, the Samoan population in the Diaspora exceeds the population of Native Samoans within the country itself. And despite many not growing up in the island life, our people have still been able to embrace their roots and proudly share it with the world.

Samoa's Influence Across Oceans

Influence in Sports

When you look at sports like Boxing, American football or rugby, you'll see so many talented athletes from the Pacific Island communities. People like Troy Polamalu and Brian To'o have shown that hard work and staying true to your roots can take you far. In 2022, Toa Samoa (Samoa's National Rugby League Team) had the world's eye on them as they were the first Pacific Island to ever make it to Grand Final of Rugby League World Cup. It's amazing to see how their culture and identity played a big role in their motivation and success. These people remind us how important it is to value teamwork and respect while chasing our dreams.

Source: Samoa Global News: Toa Samoa becomes First Pacific Island Team in a Grand Final of Rugby League World Cup

Influence in Entertainment & Social Media

In entertainment and social media, Samoans are making waves and sharing their stories with the world. Individuals like Drew Afualo, Parris Goebel and Jason Momoa just a few of the many that're out here representing their Samoan and Polynesian heritage in a way that makes people take notice. And in music, you've got artists like Opetaia Foa'i, who brought Polynesian sounds to Disney's "Moana". It's so cool to see how they use their platforms to not be afraid in celebrating their culture and continue to inspire younger generations to be proud of where they come from.
