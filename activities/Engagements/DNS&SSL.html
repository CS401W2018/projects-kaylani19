Engagement: DNS & SSL

1. What is DNS?

= DNS, the acronym for Domain Name System, is like the internet's phonebook. It translates domain names that're readable for humans into IP addresses that computers use to communicate with each other.

2. How do you get a domain name?

= To get a domain name, you need to choose a name that fits your website, business or institution and check its availability through a domain registrar like GoDaddy. Then register it by creating an account and paying the registration fee.

3. What is SSL?

SSL, short for Secure Sockets Layer, is a technology that encrypts the connection between your browser and a website, to ensure that data transmitted (like passwords or credit card information) remains secure.

4. How do you get a security certificate?

You need to choose a trusted CA (Certificate Authority) like DigiCert, you purchase it or get a free SSL certificate from the CA after they check that you own the website. Then you install the certificateon your website's server

5. How does SSL work?

It establishes a secure connection between the user's browser and the website. Then through encryption, it ensures data cannot be intercepted or altered during the interaction as well as authenticates the website's identity to prevent impersonation.

6. How do you stay safe online?

= By using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, using secure networks and more
